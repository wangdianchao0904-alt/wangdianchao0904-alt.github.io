About

I am a Project Assistant Professor at The University of Tokyo. My research focuses on accelerated carbonation of cement-based materials, recycled concrete technology, and CO₂ sequestration for low-carbon construction. I have published 46+ SCI papers, authored a monograph with Woodhead Publishing (Elsevier), and hold 29 patents across 8 countries.

I received my Ph.D. from The University of Tokyo (2019, supervised by Prof. Takafumi Noguchi), M.Eng. from Tongji University (2016, supervised by Prof. Zheng Lu), and B.Eng. from China University of Petroleum (2012).

Research

Accelerated Carbonation & CO₂ Sequestration

Developing high-temperature carbonation technology utilizing industrial waste heat to accelerate CO₂ fixation in cement-based materials. Successfully scaled from laboratory (1.3 kg/h) to pilot plant (500-1000 kg/h) with Taiheiyo Cement Corporation.

Recycled Concrete Technology

Carbonation modification of recycled aggregates to enhance mechanical properties and durability. Research on self-compacting recycled concrete, fully recycled concrete, and carbon-negative mortar systems.

Low-Carbon Construction Materials

Alkali-activated materials, geopolymer concrete, and innovative binder systems for reducing carbon footprint. Life cycle analysis and carbon emission modeling of construction materials.

Structural Vibration Control

Particle tuned mass dampers (PTMD) for wind-induced vibration control of high-rise buildings. Experimental and numerical studies on damper design and optimization.
